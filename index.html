1200 years of cherry blossom’s full-bloom dates in Kyoto, Japan

Spring 2021　By Yuriko Schumacher

This spring, cherry blossoms peaked the record earliest in Kyoto, Japan.

The visualization below shows cherry blossom's full-blooming (peak) dates since 812 in Kyoto, Japan. Data are released by Yasuyuki Aono, a professor at Osaka Prefecture University. They identified the full-blooming dates "from old documents."

Each petal represents each year's full-blooming date (converted into day of year, a number of days after January 1st). The petals pile up to formulate a histogram as you scroll down to see data for later years.

Years are encoded as colors, while angle of the petals are representations of the year's (estimated) average temperature in March. Professor Aono estimated the average temperature in ancient times, based on full-blooming dates.

Temperature data after 1880 as well as full-blooming date after 1953 are based on the official data from Japan Meteorological Agency. Data were downloaded through the agency's downloading system.
